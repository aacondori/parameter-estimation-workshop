# Phase Two Research Symposium

**A Celebration of Student Research**

## Event Overview

The Research Symposium is the culminating event of Phase Two, where student pairs present their semester-long research to an audience of peers, family, faculty, and community members. This is not just a presentation—it's a celebration of intellectual courage, persistence, and the transformation from students who consume mathematics to researchers who create it.

**Time:** 1:00 PM - 5:00 PM  
**Location:** Florida Gulf Coast University Campus  
**Format:** Poster presentations + oral presentations  
**Audience:** Students, families, FGCU faculty, high school teachers, community members

## Poster Requirements

### Dimensions and Format
- **Size:** 36" × 48" (standard research poster)
- **Orientation:** Portrait
- **Printing:** Provided by program (students submit PDF 1 week before)

### Content Sections

**1. Title and Authors**
- Research question as title
- Student names and high school
- Mentor names
- Program logo

**2. Introduction**
- Why this question matters
- Background on Newton's Law of Cooling
- What was already known

**3. Methods**
- Mathematical approach (equations, theorems)
- Computational techniques
- Data or test cases used

**4. Results**
- 2-3 key visualizations (plots, tables, diagrams)
- Main findings summarized in bullet points
- Concrete examples with numbers

**5. Discussion**
- Interpretation of results
- Connections to Phase One work
- Limitations and assumptions

**6. Conclusions and Future Work**
- Main takeaway (1-2 sentences)
- One direction for future research

**7. Acknowledgments**
- Mentors, program staff, funding sources

### Design Guidelines
- Use large fonts (title 72pt+, headings 36pt+, body 24pt+)
- Readable from 5 feet away
- Visual hierarchy (title → sections → details)
- Consistent color scheme
- Balance text and visuals (aim for 50/50)
- White space for readability

## Oral Presentation Requirements

### Slide Structure (6-8 slides)

1. **Title slide:** Question + Names
2. **Motivation:** Why this question? (1 slide)
3. **Background:** Newton's Law recap + Phase One connection (1 slide)
4. **Approach:** Key insight or method (1-2 slides)
5. **Results:** Main finding with one compelling visualization (1-2 slides)
6. **Conclusions:** Takeaway + future work (1 slide)
7. **Acknowledgments** (optional)

### Presentation Tips
- **Timing:** Practice to stay within 8 minutes (leave 2 min for Q&A)
- **Clarity:** Define all notation and terminology
- **Visuals:** Show, don't just tell (plots, diagrams, animations)
- **Examples:** Include concrete numbers from your work
- **Balance:** Both partners should speak roughly equally
- **Engagement:** Make eye contact, speak clearly
- **Backup:** Have plan if live demo fails

### Technical Setup
- Laptop connections provided (HDMI)
- Backup: Upload slides to cloud (Google Drive/OneDrive)
- Test slides on day-of before session starts

## Frequently Asked Questions

**Q: Can my family attend?**  
A: Yes! The symposium is open to all. Please invite family, teachers, and friends.

**Q: Will the event be recorded?**  
A: Yes, oral presentations will be recorded and shared with participants. Poster session photos will be taken.

**Q: What should I wear?**  
A: Business casual attire. Comfortable but professional (think: job interview or school presentation).

**Q: What if I'm nervous about presenting?**  
A: Completely normal! You'll practice multiple times with mentors and peers. The audience is supportive and friendly.

**Q: Can I list this on my resume/college applications?**  
A: Absolutely! We'll provide official documentation of your participation and accomplishments.

**Q: Will there be food?**  
A: Yes, light refreshments during the poster session. Families are welcome to dine together on campus afterward.
